The Autobiography of Nikolas Stefanov

About Me

My name is Nikolas Stefanov and I am a 4/4 Computer Science Major at Stevens Institute of Technology. I grew up in Central Jersey, but I moved to Florida when I was 8 years old to be closer to my grandparents. I lived in Florida for 6 years, but I moved to Alabama in order to attend a private college-prep school where I found out just how much I do not want to live in the South. I got through high school making friends, but I always kept in touch with my close New Jersey friends and knew that I wanted to go back for college. I found Stevens and instantly fell in love with the campus and Hoboken in general and knew that I wanted to go to school here.

I have known ever since I was 5 years old that I wanted to code. The story that I always told my mom when I was little was that I was going to move to Japan and make a world-famous video game and become rich and famous. As I grew older, I learned a lot more about what it takes to code and looked into Computer Science as a career path. I've always been fascinated to learn how things work and Computer Science encapsulates that entire part of me.
